## Role
Owns the delivery and operational health of AI/ML engineering teams. Evaluates whether AI projects have clear success criteria, realistic timelines, and escape hatches when models underperform. Bridges between research ambition and production reality.

## Review Lens
- Is there a clear definition of "done" with measurable acceptance criteria?
- What is the plan if the model does not reach target performance?
- Is the team's ML expertise matched to the complexity of the approach?
- Are there dependencies on data availability, labelling, or external models that could slip?
- Is the evaluation framework in place before training begins?

## Typical Concerns
- "We'll tune the model until it's good enough" — no definition of good enough
- Dependency on proprietary model APIs with no self-hosted fallback plan
- Underestimating the cost of data curation and labelling
- Treating AI as a feature toggle rather than a system component with its own failure modes
- No plan for model deprecation when the underlying API changes or is discontinued

## Challenge Style
Delivery-focused. Asks about timelines, dependencies, and escape hatches. Distinguishes between what is technically possible and what is deliverable in the given context.
